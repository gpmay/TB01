Call for Solutions Competition Goals

  • Bring attention to solutions that can have impact; understand their benefits and tradeoffs; create public demand for action; and, market demand for results.
  • Inspire and stimulate ambitious solutions and foster collaboration (as well as competition to market). Stimulate cross-fertilization among new and mature solutions and across industry, system and sector boundaries.
  • Create ecosystems of solutions that enable green technology to take off in a way that has impact on global systems such as climate and ocean vitality.
  • Reward exemplary solutions/technology that embody the vision of creating resiliency and planetary sustainability

 

Who we invite :

We invite inventors from established businesses, entrepreneurial ventures, research institutions and labs to showcase solutions that can scale to have a positive impact for sustainability at a planetary level

 

What we are looking for :

Solutions, technology, policy, processes or a combination of both, that:

  • Exhibit multiple benefits
  • Have systemic and game-changing impact
  • Address both mitigation and adaptation to climate change
  • Generate more good & drive less bad
  • Are scalable and adaptable to a broad range of conditions
  • Are feasible and using either current or new technology, resources and know-how
  • Can withstand empirical testing

Entry Criteria:

Entries will be submitted online and will be reviewed by:

  • a panel of experts, and
  • through crowd sourcing.

Solutions could:

  • Exhibit clarity of solution design, be game-changing, implementable and scalable
  • A part of an integrated strategy dealing with key social, economic, environmental, policy and cultural issues
  • Bold, visionary and tangible, focusing on a well-defined need of critical importance
  • Regionally specific yet globally applicable, and backed up by a solid plan and the capability to move the solution forward

Categories Overview:

Resources
  • Examples of Resources: Energy, Water, Materials, Information
  • How available resources can be used for the current generation while not over-exploiting and depleting them for the future generations
  • Solutions could demonstrate dramatically reduced resource use, green chemistry, materials substitution, de-materialization (substituting services for products)
  • Integrated solutions that address multiple resources (such as water/energy/information) and that demonstrate systemic benefits

 

Human Systems
  • Examples of Human Systems: Communications, Culture & Engagement, Human Behavior Change, Invention & Innovation, Knowledge Development & Transfer, Markets, Policies & Regulation
  • How current corporate and national strategies for managing resources can lead to environmental and social problems, such as depletion of key resources, destruction of habitat, overfishing, degradation of soil fertility.
  • Solutions should take into account the connection between resources, ecosystems and society and not consider them as separate entities.
  • Rebuilding community and economic vitality while restoring ecosystem health, for clean water, biodiversity and reuse of resources.

 

Enterprise
  • Examples of Human Systems: Communications, Culture & Engagement, Human Behavior Change, Invention & Innovation, Knowledge Development & Transfer, Markets, Policies & Regulation
  • How current corporate and national strategies for managing resources can lead to environmental and social problems, such as depletion of key resources, destruction of habitat, overfishing, degradation of soil fertility.
  • Solutions should take into account the connection between resources, ecosystems and society and not consider them as separate entities.
  • Rebuilding community and economic vitality while restoring ecosystem health, for clean water, biodiversity and reuse of resources.

 

Regional Resilience
  • Solutions that improve use of resources (water, energy, materials, information) on a regional basis.
  • Infrastructure such as smart micro grid integrating renewable energy and advanced efficiency and demand management; recycled water systems; smart transportation and land use planning that provides alternatives to single occupancy vehicles, especially for commuting; and cradle-to-cradle materials management. One name for this is EcoDistricts.
  • Multi-generational solutions that facilitate interactions among old and young, school systems that integrate service and vocational learning and connect students with adults in the community.
  • Walkable communities that combine arts, business, sports, shopping, residential and work areas and are pedestrian, transit and bicycle friendly.

 

Industry Sectors
  • Examples of Sectors: Education, Finance/Insurance/Investment, Food/Agriculture, Healthcare, Manufacturing/Supply Chain, Transportation
  • Transformation of supply chains and impact of industry on ecological health of the areas where they do business.
  • Accounting for risk and costs associated with climate change and disruption to commerce from severe weather, sea level rise and catastrophic storms.
  • Strategies for helping customers minimize environmental costs of using and disposing of products.

 

Earth Systems
  • The planet can be considered a system of systems that support living organisms, including human beings and human civilization.
  • Civilization relies on surplus value from nature (agriculture to provide food; hydrological cycle to provide water; atmosphere to provide relative climate stability; energy from the sun and from fossil fuels; minerals; lumber; oceans for food and transportation, etc.)
  • Nature provides free services such as pollination, on which at least 30% of human food production relies. The salinity of the ocean affects currents and moderates temperatures for large inhabited areas of the planet.
  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil and natural gas) and burning or clear-cutting forests has lead to acceleration in green house gas emissions levels, which is leading to warming and acidification of the oceans, warming of the earth�s atmosphere and disruption to the precipitation and weather patterns on which human agriculture and urbanization have depended.
  • Sea level rise and destruction of wetlands is leading to increased flooding and will continue to intensify as the oceans continue to warm.
  • Solutions in this area will address how to rapidly scale technology and/or behavior change to dramatically reduce GHG emissions and rebuild carbon fixing resources such as land-based forests and kelp forests.
  • Solutions may also address monitoring and modeling capabilities to help determine impacts from human activities or from other factors such as forest fires.

Rubric Overview

  • The judging of solutions will occur after the submission deadline; December 14, 2012. And will be completed by the end of February 2013.
  • Every submitted solution will be judged by experts from academic, research, business and venture communities, including experienced professors, engineers, entrepreneurs, angel and venture investors, environmental experts and other professionals.
  • Solutions will also be reviewed by members of the public through a crowd sourcing approach.

 

The submissions will be judged in the following focus areas:

  • Resources
  • Human Systems
  • Enterprise
  • Regional
  • Industry
  • Earth Systems

 

Ratings in each category will range from 0-5

0 - indicating inability to meet the basic criteria

1-2 - promising solution, could use more evidence

3-4 - strong solution with good potential for breakthrough impact

5 - outstanding solution with high potential for breakthrough impact

 

  1. Creativity - Original Technology, Innovative Application

Assessing the extent to which the work introduces a new or significantly improved solution/technology in an affordable, replicable and sustainable manner

 

  1. Approach - Problem Identification, Problem Analysis, Plan for Success

Assessing how well solutions are able to identify the problem being addressed, and how well solutions are analyzed. Additionally, assessing the short, medium and long term plan for implementation and plan to measure success of the solution

 

  1. Goals - Game Changing
    1. Multiple Categories (solution integrates a number of elements)
    2. Mitigation Strategy (solution helps reduce future global warming or pollution)
    3. Adaptation Strategy (solution helps humanity adapt to affects of global warming already in progress)
    4. Scalable (solution can be widely adopted so that impact will be significant)
    5. Reasonable Risk, Feasibility (solution is feasible to implement, may require legislation or investment or political will)
    6. Ecosystem of Solutions (solutions bring together elements that re-enforce one another, creating an ecosystem of solutions that generate a positive impact in a regenerative manner)

 

Solutions descriptions should include plans for assessing achievement of these goals.
